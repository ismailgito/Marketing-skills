# LinkedIn Post Creator

A skill for crafting high-performing LinkedIn posts for the performance marketing, D2C growth,
and digital advertising space — designed to maximize impressions, build personal brand authority,
and drive meaningful engagement through sharp hooks, real insight, and strategic hashtag selection.

Activate whenever the user:
- Asks to write, draft, improve, or repurpose content for LinkedIn

- Requests hashtag recommendations for LinkedIn
- Wants to grow impressions, reach, followers, or personal brand presence

**Trigger Command**: `"Write a LinkedIn post about [topic/idea]"` or `"Turn this into a LinkedIn post"`

---

## 3. Output

Every LinkedIn post produced by this skill must contain:

```
[HOOK LINE — 1 bold, scroll-stopping sentence]

[BODY — 3–7 short paragraphs or punchy lines, each max 2 sentences]
↳ Use line breaks between each idea
↳ Use storytelling, data, teardowns, or contrast to build tension

[CTA — 1 clear call to action: question, opinion poll, or "comment below"]

[HASHTAGS — 5 to 8 relevant hashtags, grouped at the bottom]
```

**Format Rules:**
- Maximum 1,300 characters (LinkedIn sweet spot for impressions)
- No dense paragraphs — white space is your friend
- Hook must appear in the first line (before the "see more" fold)
- End with a direct question or CTA to drive comments
- Hashtags: 2 broad reach + 2–3 niche + 1 personal brand tag

**Tone:** Sharp, real, and direct. Think: practitioner sharing actual insights from the trenches —
not a thought leader recycling Twitter threads. No corporate fluff.

## 5. Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 0 — Plan Before You Write
Before drafting, identify:
- [ ] What is the core insight, observation, or result?
- [ ] Who is the primary reader (media buyer? D2C founder? marketing student?)
- [ ] What emotion should the post trigger? (FOMO, frustration, "I needed this", curiosity)
- [ ] What action do you want the reader to take? (comment, share, DM, follow)

---

### Step 1 — Craft the Hook
Write 3 alternative hook lines. The hook must:
- Be under 12 words
- Create curiosity, provoke an emotion, or state a bold/surprising fact
- NOT start with "I" (LinkedIn penalizes ego-first openers for reach)
- Sound like something a practitioner would say — not a headline generator

**Example hooks (performance marketing context):**
- `"Most Meta ad accounts are bleeding money on this one mistake."`
- `"Your landing page isn't the problem. Your traffic quality is."`
- `"Spent ₹5L on ads. Learned more from the losses than the wins."`
- `"D2C brands obsess over CTR. The real metric is cost per purchase."`
- `"Everyone's talking about creative. Nobody's fixing their offer."`

> 🛑 **Human-in-the-Loop**: Present the 3 hook options and ask: *"Which direction fits the angle you want to take?"* Do NOT proceed without confirmation.

---

### Step 2 — Build the Body
Use one of these proven LinkedIn formats based on the topic:

| Format | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| **The Listicle** | Tips, frameworks, mistakes (e.g., "5 things killing your Meta ROAS") |
| **The Ad Teardown** | Breaking down a brand's ad strategy — what's working and why |
| **The Story Arc** | A campaign win/loss, personal learning, client result |
| **The Contrast** | Old way vs new way, common belief vs actual data |
| **The Data Drop** | Stat or benchmark + your take on what it means |
| **The Rant (Controlled)** | Industry frustration — always ends with a concrete fix |
| **The Framework** | A named system or process (e.g., "The 3-layer creative test") |

Write 3–7 punchy lines. Each line = one idea. Use `↳` or `→` bullets sparingly for lists.
Avoid explaining what you're about to say — just say it.

---

### Step 3 — Write the CTA
End with ONE of these CTA types:
- **Question**: *"What's the one metric you actually optimize for?"*
- **Opinion**: *"Agree or disagree? Drop a comment 👇"*
- **Invite**: *"If you want the full breakdown, DM me 'ROAS' and I'll share it."*
- **Tag**: *"Tag a media buyer who needs to see this."*
- **Save prompt**: *"Save this before your next campaign review."*

> 🛑 **Human-in-the-Loop**: If the post is tied to a specific campaign, lead magnet, or portfolio piece,
> confirm the CTA destination (DM, link in comments, follow) before finalizing.

---

### Step 4 — Select Hashtags
Choose 5–8 hashtags using this formula:
- **2 broad** (500K+ followers): `#DigitalMarketing` `#PerformanceMarketing`
- **2–3 niche** (10K–200K): e.g., `#MetaAds` `#D2C` `#ConversionOptimization`
- **1 topic-specific**: e.g., `#PerformanceCreative` `#SEO` `#AIMarketing` `#CopywritingTips`
- **1 trending** (check what's active in the marketing space this week if possible)

> ⚠️ Never use more than 8 hashtags. LinkedIn's algorithm deprioritizes hashtag-stuffed posts.
> Quality over quantity — pick tags where your audience actually hangs out.

---

### Step 5 — Final Review Checklist
Before delivering the post, verify:
- [ ] Hook is under 12 words and doesn't start with "I"
- [ ] Post is under 1,300 characters
- [ ] No dense paragraphs (each idea has its own line)
- [ ] CTA is clear and actionable
- [ ] 5–8 hashtags, correctly mixed (broad + niche + topic)
- [ ] Tone is natural and direct — no buzzwords, no corporate filler
- [ ] It sounds like a real practitioner wrote it, not a content template

## 6. Rules & Constraints (Edge Cases)

### ✅ Always Do
- Lead with a real insight, result, or pain point — not a vague observation
- Use white space aggressively (line breaks between every idea)
- Write for a practitioner audience — assume they know the basics
- Keep the voice consistent: confident but not arrogant, casual but not sloppy
- Offer 2–3 post variations if the topic or angle isn't fully specified

### 🚫 Never Do
- Never start the post with "I am excited to share…" or "Thrilled to announce…" (low-reach openers)
- Never use more than 8 hashtags
- Never write posts longer than 1,500 characters
- Never use hollow buzzwords without substance: "synergy", "leverage", "disruptive", "game-changer"
- Never skip the Human-in-the-Loop hook confirmation step
- Never invent campaign numbers, client names, or benchmarks — ask the user for real data
- Never write generic marketing advice that could apply to any industry — keep it channel-specific

### ⚠️ Edge Cases
| Situation | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| User shares an ad / brand to teardown | Focus on the specific creative/copy decision — what it signals about the brand's strategy |
| User wants to announce a new project or portfolio piece | Lead with the problem it solves or the result it achieved, not the announcement itself |
| User asks for a "viral" post with no topic | Ask: *"What's the insight, result, or take you want to build this around?"* |
| User wants multiple posts | Produce them one at a time with hook confirmation between each |
| Post is for the Indian market specifically | Use INR for any numbers, reference Indian D2C brands/platforms (Meesho, Zepto, Zomato, etc.) where relevant |
| User provides a long article or report to repurpose | Extract the single sharpest, most counterintuitive insight — don't summarize the whole thing |
| User wants a carousel script instead of a post | Switch to slide-by-slide format: Hook slide → 5–7 value slides → CTA slide |
